A 17-year-old boy fired shots at Detroit police officers while fleeing a traffic stop Thursday night on the city's west side, police said.

No injuries were reported after the incident at 11:05 p.m. at Evergreen and Belton, and the suspect was arrested. Officers had pulled over the 2005 Ford Escape in which the suspect was a front-seat passenger, and he exited the vehicle on foot, according to a report from police.

The two officers pursued the boy, who turned toward them and fired two shots. They did not return fire. The suspect was arrested at Auburn and Belton, two blocks east of the traffic stop, police said.

The Ford's driver, a 21-year-old man, was arrested on suspicion of driving without a license, according to the report.
